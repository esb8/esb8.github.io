## Ground Support Equipment (GSE)

### Overview  
Collaborated on the design and development of Ground Support Equipment (GSE), an electrical control cabinet for a liquid rocket engine. The system integrates flight computer interfacing, instrumentation, and actuation to enable real-time monitoring, control, and safe engine operation during testing and launch.



### Key Contributions  
- Co-designed electrical enclosure integrating **flight computer, LabJack T7, and PLC-based control systems**  
- Implemented support for **load cells, pressure transducers, and thermocouples** for real-time data acquisition  
- Developed **LabVIEW interface** for monitoring, control, and system interaction  
- Enabled control of **servos and solenoid valves** for engine actuation  
- Produced detailed wiring diagrams and system documentation covering **35+ interconnections**  
- Diagnosed critical hardware failure using **oscilloscope analysis and PCB rework (0402)**  


### System Design  

**Instrumentation & Data Acquisition**  
Integrated multiple sensor systems (pressure, force, temperature) through LabJack-based acquisition, enabling real-time monitoring during engine operation.

**Control & Actuation**  
PLC-driven control of valves and actuators, providing reliable sequencing and system response for test and launch conditions.

**System Integration**  
Designed full electrical architecture within the enclosure, including wiring, interconnects, and interfaces with the rocket engine and avionics systems.

**Debugging & Reliability**  
Resolved a critical PLC-related hardware issue through signal-level debugging and rework, restoring system functionality under test conditions.


### Results  
- Successfully supported **3+ hot-fire tests and 2 rocket launches**  
- Delivered reliable **real-time control and instrumentation** in a safety-critical environment  
- Passed **preliminary design review (PDR)** with complete system documentation
